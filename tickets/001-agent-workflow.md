## Goal

Enable users to consume machine learning research papers in audio format. The system will generate high-quality, text-to-speech-ready transcripts that make research accessible to busy individuals (e.g., parents, commuters) who want to stay current on research progress but lack time for traditional reading.

## Workflow

The system employs a multi-agent collaborative approach to ensure both accuracy and audio-optimized output:

### Phase 1: Individual Analysis
Each agent (with a distinct persona) independently reads the research paper (PDF) and produces their own notes capturing the paper's key concepts, arguments, and findings from their perspective.

### Phase 2: Collaborative Refinement
The agents engage in dialogue to synthesize their individual understandings. This discussion refines accuracy, resolves misinterpretations, and builds a shared, deeper understanding of the paper's content.

### Phase 3: Audio Transcript Generation
The agents collaborate again to produce a final transcript explicitly optimized for text-to-speech conversion. The output prioritizes:
- Clear, natural language suitable for audio narration
- Logical flow and pacing for listening
- Well-explained technical jargon (not omitted)
- Maximum educational value in spoken format

The multi-pass approach ensures the final audio content is both technically accurate and genuinely listenable.
